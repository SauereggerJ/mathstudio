title: Prove or give a counterexample (Direct Sum Uniqueness)
---

**23.) Prove or give a counterexample:**

If $V_1, V_2, U$ are subspaces of $V$ such that
$$V = V_1 \oplus U \quad \text{and} \quad V = V_2 \oplus U$$
then $V_1 = V_2$.

**Counterexample:**

Let $V = \mathbb{F}^4$, and $U = \{(x, x, y, y) \in \mathbb{F}^4 : x, y \in \mathbb{F}\}$.

Then with the previous (22) we showed:
$$V = V_1 \oplus U$$

when $V_1 = \{(x, 0, y, 0) \in \mathbb{F}^4 : x, y \in \mathbb{F}\}$, but

with $V_2 = \{(0, x, 0, y) \in \mathbb{F}^4 : x, y \in \mathbb{F}\}$ and the same argumentation
$$V = V_2 \oplus U \quad \text{also is true}$$

But $V_1 \neq V_2$.
