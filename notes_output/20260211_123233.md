# Continuity 3

**Lemma:** Suppose that $X \to \mathbb{R}$ is continuous at $x_0 \in X$ and $f(x_0) > 0$. Then there is a neighborhood $U$ of $x_0$ such that $f(x) > 0$ for all $x \in U$.

**Proof:** Set $\varepsilon := f(x_0)/2 > 0$ 
$$\Rightarrow \exists \delta > 0 : |f(x) - f(x_0)| \le |f(x) - f(x_0)| < \varepsilon = \frac{f(x_0)}{2} \quad \forall x \in B_{X}(x_0, \delta) := U$$
$$\Rightarrow f(x) > f(x_0) - \frac{f(x_0)}{2} = \frac{f(x_0)}{2} > 0.$$

**Def.:** A function $f: X \to Y$ is Lipschitz continuous with Lipschitz constant $\alpha > 0$ if
$$\mathrm{d}(f(x), f(y)) \le \alpha \mathrm{d}(x, y) \quad \forall x, y \in X.$$

**Lemma:** Every Lipschitz continuous function is continuous.

**Proof:** Let $\varepsilon > 0$. $x_0 \in X$: with $\delta := \frac{\varepsilon}{\alpha} > 0$ we have $\mathrm{d}(f(x), f(y)) \le \alpha \mathrm{d}(x, y) < \alpha \cdot \frac{\varepsilon}{\alpha} = \varepsilon$ for
$y \in B_{X}(x_0, \delta)$.

Note that, in this case $\delta$ is independent of $x_0 \in X$.

**Examples of Lipschitz continuous functions:**

1) If $E_1, \dots, E_m$ are normed vector spaces, then $E := E_1 \times \dots \times E_m$ is a normed vector space with respect to the product norm $\|\cdot\|_{\infty}$. The canonical projections $\mathrm{pr}_k: E \to E_k$, $x = (x_1, \dots, x_m) \mapsto x_k$ are Lipschitz continuous. In particular, the projections $\mathrm{pr}_k: \mathbb{K}^m \to \mathbb{K}$ are Lipschitz continuous.

**Proof:** For $x = (x_1, \dots, x_m)$ and $y = (y_1, \dots, y_m)$ we have
$$\|\mathrm{pr}_k(x) - \mathrm{pr}_k(y)\|_{E_k} = \|x_k - y_k\|_{E_k} \le \|x - y\|_{\infty}$$

2) $z \mapsto \mathrm{Re}(z)$, $z \mapsto \mathrm{Im}(z)$ and $z \mapsto \overline{z}$ is Lipschitz continuous on $\mathbb{C}$.

**Proof:** $\max \{|\mathrm{Re}(z_1) - \mathrm{Re}(z_2)|, |\mathrm{Im}(z_1) - \mathrm{Im}(z_2)|\} \le |z_1 - z_2| = |\overline{z_1} - \overline{z_2}|$.

3) Let $E$ be a normed vector space. Then the norm function $\|\cdot\|: E \to \mathbb{R}$, $x \mapsto \|x\|$ is Lipschitz continuous.

**Proof:** $\|\|x\| - \|y\|\| \le \|x - y\| \quad \forall x, y \in E$.

4) For any inner product space $(E, \langle \cdot, \cdot \rangle)$, the scalar product $\langle \cdot, \cdot \rangle: E \times E \to \mathbb{K}$ is continuous.

**Proof:** Let $(x, y), (x_0, y_0) \in E \times E$ and $\varepsilon \in (0, 1)$:
$$|\langle x, y \rangle - \langle x_0, y_0 \rangle| = |\langle x - x_0, y \rangle + \langle x_0, y - y_0 \rangle| \le |\langle x - x_0, y \rangle| + |\langle x_0, y - y_0 \rangle| \le$$
$$\|x - x_0\| \|y\| + \|x_0\| \|y - y_0\| \le \mathrm{d}((x, y), (x_0, y_0)) (\|x\| + \|x_0\|) \|y - y_0\|$$
Let $d := \max \mathrm{d}_E(x_1, y_1), (x_2, y_2)) = \max \{\|x\|, \|y\|, \|x_0\|, \|y_0\|\}$ (The product norm on $E \times E$).
Set $M := \max \{1, \|x_0\|, \|y_0\|\}$ and $\delta := \frac{\varepsilon}{(1 + 2M)}$. Then, for all $(x, y) \in B_{((x_0, y_0), \delta)}$:
$$|\langle x, y \rangle - \langle x_0, y_0 \rangle| \le \delta (2M + \delta) < \varepsilon$$
which proves the continuity of the scalar product $\langle \cdot, \cdot \rangle$ at the point $(x_0, y_0)$. $\square$

(Note: $\|f(y) - f(x)\|$ is roughly $\le C \|x - y\|$ through some bounding with $\|x - y\|$ and $\alpha$ if it is Lipschitz continuous).
