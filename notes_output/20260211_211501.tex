\textbf{13.)} Suppose $V_1,\ldots,V_m$ is linearly independent in $V$ and $W \in V$. \\ 
Show that 
$$V_1,\ldots,V_m, W \text{ linearly independent } \iff W \notin \text{span}(V_1,\ldots,V_m)$$
\section*{Solution}
\subsection*{Forward direction ($\Rightarrow$)}
This follows straight from the definitions of linearly independent.
\subsection*{Backward direction ($\Leftarrow$)}
Suppose $V_1,\ldots,V_m$ linearly independent and $W \notin \text{span}(V_1,\ldots,V_m)$.\ We assume there exist scalars $a_1,\ldots,a_m, b$ such that
$$a_1V_1 + \ldots + a_m V_m + bW = 0$$
We show $a_1 = \ldots = a_m = b = 0$.
\begin{enumerate}
    \item If $b \neq 0$:\n    $$W = -\frac{a_1}{b}V_1 - \ldots - \frac{a_m}{b}V_m$$
    This implies $W \in \text{span}(V_1,\ldots,V_m)$ in contradiction to our premise.
    \item So, $b=0$ and
    $$a_1V_1 + \ldots + a_m V_m + 0W = 0$$
    $$a_1V_1 + \ldots + a_m V_m = 0$$
    But now the linear independence of $V_1,\ldots,V_m$ implies $a_1 = \ldots = a_m = 0$.
\end{enumerate}
Thus, $a_1 = \ldots = a_m = b = 0$.
