\textbf{Banach space and Hilbert Space}

\begin{itemize}
    \item A sequence $(x_n)$ in $X$ is called a Cauchy sequence if, for each $\varepsilon > 0$, there exists a $N \in \mathbb{N}$ such that $d(x_n, x_m) < \varepsilon$ for all $m, n \geq N$.
    \item A metric space $X$ is called complete if every Cauchy sequence in $X$ converges.
    \item A complete normed vector space is called Banach space.
    \item A complete inner product space is called a Hilbert space.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Notes:}

\begin{itemize}
    \item Every convergent sequence is a Cauchy sequence.
    \item Every Cauchy sequence is bounded.
    \item If a Cauchy sequence has a convergent subsequence, then it is itself convergent.
    \item $\mathbb{R}^m$ and $\mathbb{C}^m$ are Banach spaces.
    \item Let $X$ be a nonempty set and $E = (E, || \cdot ||)$ a Banach space. Then $B(X, E)$ is also a Banach space.
\end{itemize}
