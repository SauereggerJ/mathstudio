# The Space of Bounded Functions

$X$ set, $(E, \| \cdot \|)$ a normed vector space. A function $u \in E^X$ is called bounded if the image of $u$ in $E$ is bounded.
For $u \in E^X$ define:

$$
\| u \|_{\infty} := \| u \|_{\infty, X} := \sup_{x \in X} \| u(x) \| \in \mathbb{R}^+ \cup \{\infty\}
$$

For $u \in E^X$ are equivalent:

*   $u$ is bounded
*   $u(X)$ is bounded in $E$
*   $\exists r > 0 : \| u(x) \| \leq r, \quad x \in X$
*   $\| u \|_{\infty} < \infty$

$$
B(X, E) := \{u \in E^X : u \text{ is bounded} \}
$$
is called the space of bounded functions from $X$ to $E$.

Notes:

1.  $B(X, E)$ is a subspace of $E^X$ and $\| \cdot \|_{\infty}$ is a norm, called the supremum norm, on $B(X, E)$.

2.  If $X = \mathbb{N}$, then $B(X, E)$ is the normed vector space of bounded sequences in $E$.
In the case $E = \mathbb{R}$ or $E = \mathbb{C}$:
$$
l_{\infty} := l_{\infty}(\mathbb{C}) := B(\mathbb{N}, \mathbb{C})
$$
In the normed vector space of bounded sequences with the supremum norm:
$$
\| (x_n) \|_{\infty} = \sup_{n \in \mathbb{N}} |x_n|
$$

3.  $c_0 \subseteq c \subseteq l_{\infty}$.
