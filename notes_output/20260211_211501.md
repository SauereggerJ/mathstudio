**13.)** Suppose $V_1,\ldots,V_m$ is linearly independent in $V$ and $W \in V$.

Show that

$$\left(V_1,\ldots,V_m, W \text{ linearly independent}\right) \iff \left(W \notin \text{span}\left(V_1,\ldots,V_m\right)\right)$$

**Solution:**

**Forward direction ($\Rightarrow$):**
This follows straight from the definitions of linearly independent.

**Backward direction ($\Leftarrow$):**
Suppose $V_1,\ldots,V_m$ linearly independent and $W \notin \text{span}\left(V_1,\ldots,V_m\right)$. We assume there exist scalars $a_1,\ldots,a_m, b$ such that

$$a_1V_1 + \ldots + a_m V_m + bW = 0$$

We show $a_1 = \ldots = a_m = b = 0$.

i) If $b \neq 0$:
$$W = -\frac{a_1}{b}V_1 - \ldots - \frac{a_m}{b}V_m$$
This implies $W \in \text{span}\left(V_1,\ldots,V_m\right)$ in contradiction to our premise.

ii) So, $b=0$ and
$$a_1V_1 + \ldots + a_m V_m + 0W = 0$$
$$a_1V_1 + \ldots + a_m V_m = 0$$
But now the linear independence of $V_1,\ldots,V_m$ implies $a_1 = \ldots = a_m = 0$.

Thus, $a_1 = \ldots = a_m = b = 0$.
