Let $(x_n)$ be a sequence in $\mathbb{R}$.

$$Y_n := \sup_{k \geq n} x_k := \sup \{x_k : k \geq n\}$$
$$Z_n := \inf_{k \geq n} x_k := \inf \{x_k : k \geq n\}$$

$Y_n$ and $Z_n$ are monotone sequences, so they converge in $\overline{\mathbb{R}} = \mathbb{R} \cup \{-\infty, \infty\}$.

$$\limsup_{n \to \infty} x_n := \lim_{n \to \infty} (\sup_{k \geq n} x_k) \quad \text{and}$$
$$\liminf_{n \to \infty} x_n := \lim_{n \to \infty} (\inf_{k \geq n} x_k)$$

Or,

$$\limsup_{n \to \infty} x_n = \inf_{n \in \mathbb{N}} (\sup_{k \geq n} x_k) \quad \text{and}$$
$$\liminf_{n \to \infty} x_n = \sup_{n \in \mathbb{N}} (\inf_{k \geq n} x_k)$$

**Notes:**

*   Any sequence $(x_n)$ in $\mathbb{R}$ has a smallest cluster point $x_*$ and a greatest cluster point $x^*$ in $\overline{\mathbb{R}}$ and these satisfy:

$$\liminf_{n \to \infty} x_n = x_*$$
$$\limsup_{n \to \infty} x_n = x^*$$

*   $(x_n)$ sequence in $\mathbb{R}$. Then

    $(x_n)$ converges in $\overline{\mathbb{R}}$ $\Leftrightarrow$ $\limsup_{n \to \infty} x_n \leq \liminf_{n \to \infty} x_n$

    $(x_n)$ converges in $\mathbb{R}$ $\Leftrightarrow$ $x = \lim_{n \to \infty} x_n = \limsup_{n \to \infty} x_n = \liminf_{n \to \infty} x_n$

*   (Bolzano-Weierstrass):

    Every bounded sequence in $\mathbb{R}^m$ and $\mathbb{C}^m$ has a convergent subsequence, that is, a cluster point.
