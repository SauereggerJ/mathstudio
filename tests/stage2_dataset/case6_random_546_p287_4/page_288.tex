Then, on the one hand,
$$\omega^2_B(e_2, e_3) := \mathrm{d}V(B, e_2, e_3) = \sum_{i=1}^3 B^i \mathrm{d}V(e_i, e_2, e_3) = B^1 \cdot \langle e_1, e_2, e_3 \rangle = B^1,$$
where $\mathrm{d}V$ is the volume element in $T\mathbb{R}^3_t$ see (14.28) and (14.27)).
On the other hand, by (14.30) we obtain
$$\omega^2_B(e_2, e_3) = (b_1 \mathrm{d}t^2 \wedge \mathrm{d}t^3 + b_2 \mathrm{d}t^3 \wedge \mathrm{d}t^1 + b_3 \mathrm{d}t^1 \wedge \mathrm{d}t^2) (e_2, e_3)$$
$$= b_1 \mathrm{d}t^2 \wedge \mathrm{d}t^3 \langle e_2, e_3 \rangle = \frac{b_1}{\sqrt{E_2 E_3}}.$$
Comparing these results, we conclude that $b_1 = B^1\sqrt{E_2 E_3}$. Similarly, we verify
that $b_2 = B^2\sqrt{E_1 E_3}$ and $b_3 = B^3\sqrt{E_1 E_2}$.
Thus we have found the representation
$$\omega^2_B = B^1\sqrt{E_2 E_3} \mathrm{d}t^2 \wedge \mathrm{d}t^3 + B^2\sqrt{E_3 E_1} \mathrm{d}t^3 \wedge \mathrm{d}t^1 + B^3\sqrt{E_1 E_2} \mathrm{d}t^1 \wedge \mathrm{d}t^2 =$$
$$\omega^2_B = \sqrt{E_1 E_2 E_3} \left( \frac{B^1}{\sqrt{E_1}} \mathrm{d}t^2 \wedge \mathrm{d}t^3 + \frac{B^2}{\sqrt{E_2}} \mathrm{d}t^3 \wedge \mathrm{d}t^1 + \frac{B^3}{\sqrt{E_3}} \mathrm{d}t^1 \wedge \mathrm{d}t^2 \right) \quad \text{(14.32)}$$
